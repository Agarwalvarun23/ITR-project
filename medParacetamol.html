Paracetamol



  • Paracetamol, also known by its chemical name acetaminophen, is one of the most widely used medications for relieving pain and reducing fever. It is commonly available over-the-counter and is a key ingredient in many cold and flu remedies, as well as standalone pain relief tablets and liquid suspensions. Paracetamol is effective in treating a variety of conditions, including headaches, muscle aches, arthritis, toothaches, and menstrual cramps.

  • The mechanism by which paracetamol works involves inhibiting the production of prostaglandins in the brain. Prostaglandins are chemicals in the body that promote inflammation, pain, and fever. By blocking their synthesis, paracetamol helps to alleviate these symptoms. Unlike nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen and aspirin, paracetamol does not have significant anti-inflammatory properties, making it less effective for conditions where inflammation is a primary concern.
  • One of the advantages of paracetamol is its relatively low risk of gastrointestinal side effects compared to NSAIDs. It does not cause stomach ulcers or gastrointestinal bleeding, which can be a concern with long-term use of NSAIDs. However, paracetamol is not without its risks. The primary danger associated with paracetamol is its potential to cause liver damage, particularly when taken in high doses or in combination with alcohol. The recommended maximum daily dose for adults is generally 4 grams, but even slightly exceeding this amount can lead to severe liver injury or acute liver failure.
  • Paracetamol is metabolized in the liver, where it is broken down into both harmless and potentially harmful metabolites. In normal doses, the liver can safely process these metabolites, but in large amounts, the toxic metabolite N-acetyl-p-benzoquinone imine (NAPQI) can accumulate, leading to liver damage. Symptoms of paracetamol overdose can include nausea, vomiting, sweating, and confusion, and prompt medical treatment is essential to mitigate the effects.
  • Overall, paracetamol is a valuable and widely used medication for managing pain and fever. When used responsibly and according to guidelines, it is safe and effective for most people. However, awareness of its potential risks, particularly regarding liver health, is crucial to ensure its safe use.
